Biography

Ahmad Nabha is a versatile actor, director, and theatre educator with over 15 years of experience in both Arabic and international theatre. Born in Damascus and trained at the prestigious Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts in London, Ahmad brings a unique cross-cultural perspective to his work.

His career spans classical and contemporary theatre, with notable performances in Shakespeare, Chekhov, and modern Arabic drama. As a director, Ahmad is known for his innovative interpretations that bridge Eastern and Western theatrical traditions.

Currently serving as a theatre instructor at the National Arts Academy, Ahmad is passionate about nurturing the next generation of performers. His teaching methodology combines classical techniques with contemporary approaches, emphasizing emotional truth and cultural authenticity.

Ahmad's work has been recognized with several awards, including Best Actor at the Middle East Film Festival and the Excellence in Theatre Education Award from the Arab Theatre Union.
